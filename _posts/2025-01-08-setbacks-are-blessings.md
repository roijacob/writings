I got laid off at my first job. A pretty good head start for a fresh graduate who doesn't know any better. I was so caught off guard because I knew that I was doing well, I'm competent, and my manager sees it too. But the company had to retrench to remain operational, and it was all understandable.

Throughout the time that I was finding a new job, my confidence was at its lowest. I thought that it would take me a good 2 months or more to find a job. Then, one company gave me a chance (I got in less than a month after the notice), and it turned out to be what I had dreamed of in the back of my mind.

- The problem domain is worth penetrating. 
- We were equipped with a good tech stack for the job. 
- Collaboration is an essential part of work. 
- Time logging is not strict. 
- We were small enough to have good exposure to almost everything (tools, processes, problems, etc.).

These are the aspects of my work that improved by moving to this new company, all because I was laid off. This opportunity would not be present for my taking if that setback did not happen to me.

I am so thankful for the man above for blessing me. Throughout my job hunt, I trust my faith in Him to guide me on what seems like a blurry path on my end because I really don't know how companies would accept me if they see that short stint on my resume, as a fresh grad who got laid off and is yet to gain his experience.

If you are reading this and also experiencing a setback of some sort, know that it is only temporary and you will be fine; just don't give up. You got this!

<br>

I'm already in my first month at this new company, and I can say that it is way better than my first job, by a factor of >10. Hoping to get regularized. 😊
